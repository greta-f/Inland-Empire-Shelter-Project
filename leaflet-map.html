Shelter Distribution Map

This map visualizes the distribution of animal shelters across Riverside and San Bernardino Counties. Clustering highlights areas with higher concentrations of shelters, particularly in more urbanized regions of the Inland Empire. In contrast, rural and desert areas show fewer shelters, indicating potential gaps in accessibility and resource availability.

Click on the paw prints to explore individual shelter locations, view address information, and access directions through Google Maps.


Although Inland Valley Humane Society is located in Pomona within Los Angeles County, its geographic proximity to the Inland Empire allows it to serve populations in both Riverside and San Bernardino Counties, highlighting the importance of cross-county resource networks.

Shelter Coverage Changes further burdens San Bernardino City Shelter

Beginning July 1, 2024, the Riverside County Department of Animal Services discontinued sheltering services for the cities of Colton, Fontana, and Rialto. Responsibility for animals from these areas was transferred to San Bernardino City Shelter. Although this transition aligns with county boundaries, it places additional pressure on a system already experiencing high intake levels. This shift created an increase on an already overcrowded shelter with limited resources and lack of funding.

These changes highlight how administrative boundaries and policy decisions directly shape the spatial distribution of services and demand.
