The Sign of a Crisis

Key Insights

The graph above compares the top five animal shelter intake totals by state for 2024 and 2025. In 2024, Texas had the highest intake numbers, with California ranking second. By 2025, California overtook Texas, reporting the highest intake total in the United States. Although both states have large populations that help explain higher intake volumes, the magnitude of these figures remains troubling. The increase in intake numbers reflects growing strain on shelter systems, suggesting that population size alone does not account for the challenges of managing animal welfare and capacity.

Shelters in these two states are experiencing sustained strain, with intake often outpacing adoption and transfer rates. These trends help explain the overcrowding conditions observed in many facilities in California and Texas and highlight the broader challenges facing animal shelter systems nationwide.

Riverside County Animal Shelter Data 2025 Intakes vs Outcomes

Outcome Types and Animal Welfare Trends

This dashboard above highlights monthly shelter trends for Riverside County Animal Services in 2025, comparing intake and outcomes for both dogs and cats while also examining overall overcrowding pressure. The data reveals clear seasonal patterns, with intake increasing during the late spring and summer months. During these periods, total outcomes—which include adoptions, transfers, and non-live outcomes such as euthanasia— often do not keep pace with incoming animals, resulting in positive net intake values and increased overcrowding. While some stabilization occurs later in the year, these trends underscore the ongoing strain on shelter capacity and highlight the importance of adoptions, rescue partnerships, and preventative efforts such as spay and neuter programs. While total outcomes include both positive placements and non-live outcomes, increasing live outcomes such as adoptions and transfers is critical to reducing overcrowding without reliance on euthanasia.
